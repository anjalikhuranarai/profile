About Me

Upcoming: Research Intern at Microsoft Research FATE Lab, New York (May – August 2025)

Collaboration: Ongoing research collaboration with Meta Reality Labs

I I am a 4th-year Ph.D. candidate in the School of Computing Science at Simon Fraser University and an incoming Research Intern at Microsoft Research FATE, New York. I work on Human-AI Interaction, Transparency and Control and Software Help-Seeking to design and develop LLM-based assistants, copilots for software help-seeking and AR/mixed reality applications, which were specifically designed to foster effective human-agent collaboration and help users build accurate mental models when interacting with these novel paradigms. My research has been published as a full paper in top-tier HCI and AI conferences: CHI 2025, IUI 2024, UBICOMP 2024, VL/HCC 2021. In my PhD research, I was fortunate to collaborate with Meta Reality Labs, Stanford University, and ETH Zurich. I enjoy working on the design space (iterative design and prototyping, user-centered design approach), and applying LLM models to cater to software user needs. Before coming to SFU, I interned in product development in Atlanta, USA, and a two-month technical mentorship program at IIT Hyderabad.
